Depression Cake

Description

Depression Cake is the perfect dessert for when life gives you absolutely nothing, and you still feel the need to pretend things are fine. Born during the Great Depression, this culinary masterpiece was whipped up by people who had no butter, no milk, no eggs, and frankly, no hope—just an iron will and some baking soda. It’s the cake equivalent of putting on a nice outfit to cry on the couch.

Made from whatever was still in the pantry after the economy imploded: flour, sugar, vinegar, a splash of oil, and maybe a little cocoa if things weren’t too bleak, Depression Cake manages to be moist, slightly sweet, and emotionally hollow. It’s easy, affordable, and best of all, doesn’t require you to feel joy to make it. Great for when the stock market crashes, rent is due, or you’re simply out of eggs and the will to live.

Pairs beautifully with a hot cup of boiled water.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • ⅓ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup water

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In an 8x8 inch (or similar-sized) ungreased baking pan or mixing bowl, whisk together:
    • flour, sugar, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Use a spoon to make three wells in the dry mixture:
    • Pour vanilla in one well, vinegar in another, and oil in the third.
  4. Pour water over everything and mix until just combined.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  6. Cool completely before serving. Dust with powdered sugar or top with frosting if you like!

Optional Toppings:

  • Powdered sugar
  • Chocolate frosting
  • Whipped cream
  • Fresh berries
  • Ice cream :)
