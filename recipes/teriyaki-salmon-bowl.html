Teriyaki Salmon Bowl

Description

These Teriyaki Salmon Rice Bowls are a vibrant and satisfying meal that brings together tender chunks of pan-seared salmon with a rich, glossy teriyaki glaze. The salmon is lightly crisped on the outside while staying juicy and flaky inside, coated in a savory-sweet sauce made from soy sauce, honey, garlic, and ginger. Each bite is packed with umami and balanced flavor, making it a perfect protein to serve over fluffy white rice or your grain of choice.

To round out the bowl, it's paired with a refreshing cucumber and avocado salad that adds coolness and creaminess to the dish. The crispness of the cucumber contrasts beautifully with the buttery texture of the avocado, bringing a fresh, light component that balances the rich salmon. Together, these elements create a well-rounded bowl thatâ€™s easy to put together on a weeknight but impressive enough to serve to guests.

Ingredients

For Salmon Bites

  • 1.5 lbs salmon fillet (skin removed)
  • 1/2 cup teriyaki sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • salt and pepper (to taste)

For Avocado Cucumber Salad

  • 2 ripe avocadoes (diced)
  • 1 large cucumber (sliced in half moons)
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • salt and pepper (to taste)

For Sriracha Mayo

  • 1/3 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons sriracha sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • salt (to taste)

To Serve

  • 2 cups cooked rice (ideally Jasmine or Basmati)
  • sesame seeds (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Cut the salmon into bite-sized cubes. In a mixing bowl, combine teriyaki sauce, olive or sesame oil, cornstarch, salt, and pepper. Whisk until smooth. Add salmon cubes into the bowl and gently mix until they're fully coated. Marinate for at least 20 minutes.
  2. Preheat your air fryer to 400°F (200°C). Place the salmon bites in a single layer in the air fryer basket, ensuring they aren't touching. You may need to cook them in batches. Cook for 7-10 minutes, turning them halfway, or until the salmon is cooked through and has a slightly crispy exterior.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine diced avocados, sliced cucumber, rice vinegar, sesame oil, salt, and pepper. Toss gently until everything is well-mixed. Set aside.
  4. In a small bowl, combine mayonnaise, sriracha sauce, lemon or lime juice, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Serve cooked rice on each plate, followed by a portion of the air-fried salmon bites. Add a scoop of avocado cucumber salad beside the salmon. Drizzle the sriracha mayo over the salmon or serve on the side. Garnish with black sesame seeds.
